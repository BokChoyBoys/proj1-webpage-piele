Explain how these bounce numbers relate to the particular effects that appear. Make sure to include all screenshots.

For the reflection sphere, the reflection looks like scene from the previous ray depth or ray depth - 1. This is because the reflection seen from the camera requires another bounce since we are reflecting which makes the reflection on the sphere look like ray depth - 1. For the refraction sphere, the light has to have an extra bounce to leave the sphere so that is why we can fully see the refraction at m=3 when the reflection was visible at m=2. We see the refraction on the right blue wall when m=4 since light exiting the sphere require 3 bounces to be seen by the camera so bouncing off the wall would require another bounce hence m=4. This is why the light under the sphere is visible at m=3 since light exits the sphere with two bounces and then bounces to the camera.

Part 4. Depth of Field

For these subparts, we recommend using a microfacet BSDF scene to show off the cool out of focus effects you can get with depth of field!

In a few sentences, explain the differences between a pinhole camera model and a thin-lens camera model.

Pinhole camera model allows light to only go into the camera in one direction which causes the subject to always be in focus. Thin lens camera model refract incoming light which makes it come in different directions. Only at the focal length plane is when the object is in focus.
